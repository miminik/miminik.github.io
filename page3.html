Sand Point Well: Sustainable Water Extraction for Resilient Living

Introduction

Access to clean water is a cornerstone of energy and environmental sustainability. In regions where surface water is available nearby, Sand Point Wells—also known as driven point wells—offer an inexpensive, low-energy alternative to deep drilling and piped water infrastructure. This exhibit introduces a hands-on exploration of how these wells work and how they can secure water independence while protecting groundwater resources.

Exhibit Concept

This exhibit features a transparent cross-sectional model of a sand point well setup near a simulated riverbank. Visitors can observe how the pointed screen is manually driven into the saturated zone and how clean water is drawn using a hand or solar-powered pump. LED indicators show water table depth, flow rate, and filtration quality. Infographics highlight its energy efficiency and potential for rural and emergency water supply.

Interactive Element

Visitors can engage in a digital simulation where they choose a landscape, identify water table depth, and attempt to install a sand point well. They will compare results based on terrain, soil type, and proximity to surface water. A real hand pump demo will allow physical interaction, while on-screen analytics show energy consumed per litre and water quality index.

As per Wikipedia and reports from the International Groundwater Resources Assessment Centre (IGRAC), sand point wells are ideal for temporary or long-term use in flood-prone, rural, or off-grid areas. They require minimal machinery and can be maintained without external energy dependency.

Research & Groundwater Relevance

Groundwater accounts for nearly 30% of the world's freshwater. Shallow aquifers are especially vulnerable to over-extraction and contamination. According to the Central Ground Water Board of India, decentralised, low-impact solutions like sand point wells are crucial in conserving water tables and ensuring local water resilience. They also support sustainable agriculture in peri-urban and rural zones.
